Informatics Computing and Information Systems

University Oxford Brookes University (UK)
Course Objectives This course combines some of the generality of the computing course with modules concerned with the development of information systems. An information system is a set of people, data, procedures, hardware and software that work together to achieve the common goal of information management. Information management includes the tasks of gathering data; processing the data into reliable, accurate, and usable information; and distributing the information in a timely fashion for use in decision making, problem solving, and control. Information systems exist within organisations in commerce, industry and the public sector. Therefore, while it is primarily a computing discipline, it involves an understanding of organisations, management, information and the information needs of organizations.

  • Systems Analysis & Design
  • Software Engineering

  • Relational Database Design
  • Advanced User Interfaces
  • Information Systems Design
  • Software Project Management
  • Management Information Systems
Duration Diploma (8 mths) + Advanced Dip (8 mths) + Degree (12 mths)
Course Fee S$31800 + S$750.00 (Registration Fee)
All fees quoted are exclusive of 7% GST. The above fee covers all lectures, course notes, class handouts, student fee, library fee, examination fee but exclude textbooks and repeat course fee.
Admission Criteria
  • Minimum 16 years old
  • ‘A’ Levels or equivalent conducted with English
  • ‘A’ Levels or equivalent conducted without English (Must do English Communication)
  • Minimum 3 GCE ‘O’ Levels or equivalent with English (Must do CT and LS as enrichment modules)
  • Minimum 3 GCE ‘O’ Levels or equivalent without English (Must do English Communication, then Critical Thinking and Learning Skills as enrichment modules)
Additional Criteria Entrants whose main language is English, or who studied for their diploma and advanced diploma on courses where the language of instruction was English, are deemed to satisfy the English-language requirement. All other entrants must hold one of the current Brookes-recognised English-language qualifications (IELTS 6.0 or TOEFL 550 or equivalent).
